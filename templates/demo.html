International Morse code is composed of five elements: dot: short mark, dot or dit: one time unit long. dash: long mark, dash or dah: three time units long. inter gap: inter-element gap between the dots and dashs within a character: one unit long. short gap: short gap (between letters): three time units long. medium gap: medium gap (between words): seven time units long. a `time unit` in this context is a letter. these five elements will be referred to as `configuration` when describing multiple set of elements will be referred to as `configurations`
